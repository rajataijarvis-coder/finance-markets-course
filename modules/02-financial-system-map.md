**Learning time:** 20-25 minutes  
**Prerequisites:** Module 1 (What is Money?)

---

## 🗺️ The Big Picture

Now that we understand money, let's see how it **flows** through the economy.

Think of the financial system like your body's circulatory system:
- **Heart** = Central bank (pumps money)
- **Arteries** = Banks (channel money)
- **Capillaries** = Financial markets (distribute money)
- **Cells** = Businesses and people (use money)

---

## 🔄 The Two Sides of Finance

### SAVERS (Have Extra Money)
- You and me with savings
- Companies with profits
- Pension funds
- Foreign investors

**They want:** Safe place to store money + earn returns

### BORROWERS (Need Money)
- Someone buying a house
- A startup needing capital
- Government building infrastructure
- Company expanding factories

**They need:** Access to capital to do things

## 🏦 Banks: The Original Intermediaries

### What Banks Do

**1. Take Deposits (Liability)**
- You deposit ₹1,00,000
- Bank owes you ₹1,00,000 (this is their liability)
- You can withdraw anytime

**2. Make Loans (Asset)**
- Bank lends ₹90,000 to a business
- Business owes bank ₹90,000 + interest (this is bank's asset)
- Business pays back over time

**3. Facilitate Payments**
- Transfer money between accounts
- Process checks, cards, UPI
- Enable trade

### The Spread (How Banks Make Money)

| Activity | Rate | Example |
|----------|------|---------|
| Pay you (deposit interest) | 4% | ₹1,00,000 × 4% = ₹4,000/year |
| Charge borrower (loan interest) | 10% | ₹90,000 × 10% = ₹9,000/year |
| **Bank's profit (spread)** | **6%** | **₹5,000/year** |

## 📈 Markets: Where Buyers Meet Sellers

### Stock Market (Equity)
**What trades:** Ownership in companies (shares)

**Example:**
- Tata Motors needs ₹10,000 crore to build electric cars
- Instead of borrowing, they sell shares
- You buy 100 shares at ₹500 each
- You now own a tiny piece of Tata Motors
- If they succeed, your shares become worth more

### Bond Market (Fixed Income)
**What trades:** Loans (IOUs)

**Example:**
- Indian government needs ₹1 lakh crore for highways
- They issue "government bonds"
- You buy a ₹1,00,000 bond
- Government promises to pay you back in 10 years + 7% interest yearly
- You lent money to the government

### Forex Market (Currency)
**What trades:** Currencies

**Example:**
- Indian company needs to buy $1 million of US software
- They need rupees → dollars
- Forex market matches them with someone who has dollars → wants rupees

### Money Market
**What trades:** Short-term borrowing (overnight to 1 year)

**Example:**
- HDFC Bank has extra cash today
- ICICI Bank needs cash today
- They trade in the money market (repurchase agreements, commercial paper)

---

## 🎯 Investment Funds: Pooled Money

### Mutual Funds
- Many people pool money
- Professional manager invests it
- You own "units" of the fund
- Types: Equity funds, debt funds, hybrid funds

### ETFs (Exchange-Traded Funds)
- Like mutual funds but trade on stock exchange
- Track an index (Nifty 50, Sensex)
- Lower fees

### Pension Funds
- Long-term savings for retirement
- Huge pools of capital
- Major market participants

### Hedge Funds
- For wealthy investors
- Can use complex strategies
- Aim for absolute returns

## 🏛️ Central Banks: The System's Heart

### Reserve Bank of India (RBI)
**Role:** India's central bank

**What it does:**
- Issues rupees (only RBI can create rupees)
- Sets interest rates (Repo rate)
- Regulates banks
- Manages foreign exchange reserves
- Controls inflation

### Federal Reserve (Fed) — US
**Role:** America's central bank

**Why it matters globally:**
- US dollar is world's reserve currency
- Fed decisions affect everyone
- When Fed raises rates, money flows to US

### European Central Bank (ECB)
**Role:** Eurozone's central bank

## 🔀 How Money Actually Flows

### Example: You Save ₹10,000

```
You deposit ₹10,000 in HDFC Bank
           ↓
HDFC keeps ₹2,500 as reserve (with RBI)
           ↓
HDFC lends ₹7,500 to a startup
           ↓
Startup builds solar panels
           ↓
Startup sells panels, earns revenue
           ↓
Startup repays loan + interest
           ↓
HDFC pays you interest
           ↓
You withdraw or reinvest
```

**Your ₹10,000 helped build solar infrastructure!**

## 📊 Market vs. Bank Financing

| Feature | Bank Loans | Markets (Stocks/Bonds) |
|---------|-----------|------------------------|
| Who decides | Bank manager | Market (many investors) |
| Relationship | Long-term | Often anonymous |
| Amount | Limited by bank's capital | Potentially unlimited |
| Speed | Weeks/months | Hours/days |
| Cost | Interest rate | Market price |
| Best for | Small-medium businesses, individuals | Large companies, governments |

---

## 💡 Key Takeaways

1. **Financial system connects savers to borrowers** — without it, growth stalls

2. **Banks are intermediaries** — they transform short-term deposits into long-term loans

3. **Markets are also intermediaries** — they match buyers and sellers directly

4. **Different markets serve different needs:**
   - Stocks = ownership/wealth creation
   - Bonds = stable income/lending
   - Forex = currency exchange
   - Money market = short-term liquidity

5. **Central banks oversee everything** — they control the money supply and set the rules
