## 🤔 The Big Picture

The **foreign exchange market** (Forex or FX) is the largest financial market in the world — trading over $7 trillion every single day. That's more than all stock markets combined.

Every time you exchange pounds for euros at the airport, or when an international company pays overseas suppliers, you're participating in the forex market.

---

## 🌍 Why Exchange Rates Matter

### In Your Daily Life

```
EXCHANGE RATES IN ACTION
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────
🇬🇧 UK → 🇺🇸 US Trip:
• You exchange £1,000 at GBP/USD 1.27
• You receive: $1,270

🇬🇧 UK → 🇪🇺 Europe:
• You exchange £500 at GBP/EUR 1.17
• You receive: €585

If GBP strengthens: Your money buys MORE foreign goods
If GBP weakens: Your money buys LESS foreign goods
```

### For Businesses

```
UK COMPANY IMPORTING FROM US
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Order: $100,000 of components
Invoice date: 1 GBP = 1.27 USD → Pay £78,740
Payment date:  1 GBP = 1.20 USD → Pay £83,333

→ Currency moved against them → £4,593 EXTRA cost
→ This is why companies hedge forex risk!
```

## 📊 How Forex Works

### Currency Pairs

Every forex trade involves a **pair** — you're always buying one currency while selling another:

```
CURRENCY PAIRS EXPLAINED
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  GBP/USD (British Pound / US Dollar)                │
│  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────  │
│  GBP = Base currency (what you're buying)           │
│  USD = Quote currency (what you're paying)          │
│  Quote: 1.27 means £1 = $1.27                      │
│                                                       │
│  EUR/GBP (Euro / British Pound)                     │
│  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────  │
│  EUR = Base currency                                │
│  GBP = Quote currency                               │
│  Quote: 0.85 means €1 = £0.85                      │
│                                                       │
│  USD/JPY (US Dollar / Japanese Yen)                 │
│  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────  │
│  USD = Base currency                                │
│  JPY = Quote currency                               │
│  Quote: 150 means $1 = ¥150                         │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

### Major Currency Pairs

| Pair | Nickname | Daily Volume | Description |
|------|----------|--------------|-------------|
| **EUR/USD** | "Eurodollar" | 28% | Most traded — controls the market |
| **GBP/USD** | "Cable" | 12% | British pound vs dollar |
| **USD/JPY** | "Yen" | 17% | Dollar vs Japanese yen |
| **USD/CHF** | "Swissy" | 5% | Dollar vs Swiss franc |
| **AUD/USD** | "Aussie" | 6% | Australian dollar |
| **USD/CAD** | "Loonie" | 5% | Canadian dollar |

### Cross Currency Pairs

```
CROSS PAIRS (No USD involved)
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• GBP/EUR — British pound vs Euro
• EUR/JPY — Euro vs Japanese yen
• EUR/CHF — Euro vs Swiss franc
• AUD/NZD — Australian vs New Zealand dollar
```

## 💱 Reading Forex Quotes

```
BROKER QUOTE DISPLAY
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────
         BID        ASK       SPREAD
        ─────      ─────     ──────
GBP/USD  1.2705    1.2708    0.0003
EUR/USD  1.0850    1.0853    0.0003
USD/JPY  149.85    149.88    0.03

Key terms:
• BID   = Price to SELL the base currency
• ASK   = Price to BUY the base currency  
• SPREAD = Difference (broker's fee)

Example: Buy £10,000 against USD
────────────────────────────────────
At ask of 1.2708: You pay $12,708
If GBP strengthens to 1.2800:
  Your £10,000 now = $12,800
  Profit: $92 (0.72%)
```

## 📈 What Moves Exchange Rates?

### 1. Interest Rates

Central bank rates are the biggest driver:

```
UK INTEREST RATES & GBP
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
2021: BoE rates 0.1% → GBP/USD 1.40
2023: BoE rates 5.25% → GBP/USD 1.25

→ Higher UK rates = stronger GBP (usually)
→ But only relative to other countries!
```

### 2. Inflation

Higher inflation erodes currency value:

```
INFLATION EXAMPLE (Europe)
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
2022: ECB raises rates to fight 10% inflation
Result: EUR strengthened initially

But long-term: High inflation → weaker currency
because it erodes purchasing power
```

### 3. Trade Balance

Exports vs imports matter:

```
UK TRADE BALANCE
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Trade deficit = GBP weakens (importing more than exporting)
• Trade surplus = GBP strengthens

UK: Historically trade deficit
→ Structural pressure on GBP over decades
```

### 4. Political Stability

Uncertainty drives currency moves:

```
POLITICAL RISK EXAMPLES
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Brexit referendum 2016: GBP fell 15% in weeks
• UK political chaos 2022: GBP hit all-time low vs USD
• US elections: USD typically strengthens
```

### 5. Market Sentiment

```
RISK ON / RISK OFF
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Risk ON (confidence high):
• Investors buy higher-yielding currencies
• GBP, AUD, CAD strengthen

Risk OFF (fear high):
• Investors flee to "safe havens"
• USD, JPY, CHF strengthen
```

## 🏛️ The Forex Market Structure

### Not a Central Exchange

Unlike stocks, forex trades **over-the-counter (OTC)** — directly between banks and traders:

```
FOREX MARKET STRUCTURE
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│              Interbank Market                      │
│         (Banks trading with banks)                │
│              ~$5 trillion/day                     │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
                        │
         ┌──────────────┼──────────────┐
         ▼              ▼              ▼
    ┌─────────┐   ┌──────────┐   ┌──────────┐
    │Corpora- │   │  Retail  │   │Central   │
    │tions    │   │  Brokers │   │Banks     │
    │(Hedge)  │   │(e.g., FX)│   │(MARKETS) │
    └─────────┘   └──────────┘   └──────────┘
```

### Key Players

| Participant | Role |
|-------------|------|
| **Central banks** | Set policy, hold reserves |
| **Commercial banks** | Major market makers |
| **Corporations** | Hedge international trade |
| **Hedge funds** | Speculate for profit |
| **Retail brokers** | Individual access |
| **Payment providers** | Currency conversion |

### 24-Hour Market

```
FOREX TRADING HOURS (UTC)
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Session       │ Hours (UTC)    │ Key Markets
──────────────┼────────────────┼────────────
Sydney        │ 22:00-07:00    │ Australia, NZ
Tokyo         │ 00:00-09:00    │ Japan, Hong Kong
London        │ 08:00-17:00    │ UK, Europe
New York      │ 13:00-22:00    │ US, Americas
Overlap       │ 13:00-17:00    │ Most volatile!

→ London + New York overlap = most liquid
```

## 📉 Types of Forex Orders

### Market Orders

```
BUY GBP/USD AT MARKET
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Click "Buy"
• Executed immediately at current ask price
• Used when you want in NOW
```

### Limit Orders

```
SET GBP/USD LIMIT ORDER AT 1.2500
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Set order to buy if price drops to 1.2500
• Executes automatically when reached
• Used for better pricing or waiting
```

### Stop-Loss Orders

```
SET GBP/USD STOP-LOSS AT 1.2600
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• If price falls to 1.2600
• Automatic sell triggers
• Limits your losses if trade goes wrong
```

## ⚠️ Key Risks in Forex

### 1. Leverage Risk (MAJOR)

Forex brokers offer **massive leverage**:

```
LEVERAGE EXAMPLE
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────
100:1 leverage means:
• You control £100,000 with just £1,000

If price moves 1% AGAINST you:
• £1,000 → £0 (100% loss!)
• You've lost your entire margin
```

> ⚠️ **Warning:** Most retail forex traders lose money. Leverage amplifies both wins and losses.

### 2. Central Bank Policy

Unexpected rate changes cause massive moves:

```
SNB SURPRISE (January 2015)
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Swiss National Bank removed EUR/CHF floor
• In minutes, CHF surged 30%
• Many retailers wiped out
• Some brokers went bankrupt
```

### 3. Liquidity Risk

In extreme events, liquidity disappears:

```
COVID CRASH (March 2020)
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• GBP/USD fell 10% in weeks
• Some retail brokers couldn't fill orders
• Spreads widened dramatically
```

### 4. Counterparty Risk

Your broker might not pay:

```
PROTECT YOURSELF
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Use FCA-regulated brokers (UK)
• Check broker segregation of funds
• Don't keep funds too long
• Consider negative balance protection
```

## 💡 Key Takeaways

1. **Largest market in the world** — $7+ trillion daily (far bigger than stocks)

2. **Currency pairs** — You're always buying one, selling another

3. **Major pairs include EUR/USD, GBP/USD, USD/JPY** — These drive the market

4. **Rates drive prices** — Central bank interest rates are the biggest factor

5. **Leverage is dangerous** — 100:1 is common; 1% move can wipe you out

6. **24/5 market** — Forex trades around the clock except weekends
