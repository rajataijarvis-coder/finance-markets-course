# Module 14: Credit Markets

**Learning time:** 20-25 minutes  
**Prerequisites:** Modules 6-13 (Equity, Fixed Income, Derivatives)

## 🤔 The Big Picture

While **bonds** are traded securities, **credit markets** are about private debt — bank loans, syndicated financing, and the analysis of whether borrowers can pay. This is where "credit risk" is actually created and managed.

Credit markets are larger than stock markets and far more opaque. Most corporate financing comes from banks, not bonds.

---

## 📖 What Are Credit Markets?

### Definition

Credit markets encompass **private borrowing** — loans and debt that aren't traded on exchanges:

```
CREDIT MARKETS VS BOND MARKETS
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────
┌───────────────────┬──────────────────┬──────────────────┐
│ Feature           │ Credit Markets   │ Bond Markets     │
├───────────────────┼──────────────────┼──────────────────┤
│ Trading           │ Mostly private   │ Exchange traded  │
│ Disclosure       │ Limited          │ Heavily required │
│ Relationships    │ Ongoing          │ Arm's length     │
│ Flexibility       │ High             │ Standardized     │
│ Documentation     │ Custom           │ Uniform          │
│ Information       │ Private          │ Public           │
└───────────────────┴──────────────────┴──────────────────┘
```

### Types of Credit

| Type | Description | Typical Users |
|------|-------------|---------------|
| **Term loans** | Fixed maturity, regular payments | Corporations, real estate |
| **Revolving credit** | Draw down and repay | Working capital |
| **Syndicated loans** | Multiple banks lend to one borrower | Large corporates |
| **Securitized debt** | Pooled and sold as securities | Mortgages, auto loans |
| **Trade credit** | Supplier financing | Suppliers/customers |

## 🏦 Bank Lending

### How Bank Credit Works

```
CORPORATE LOAN PROCESS
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────
1. Company approaches bank for £10M loan
2. Bank analyzes credit (3 Cs):
   • Character (management quality)
   • Capital (leverage, equity)
   • Capacity (cash flow to repay)
3. Bank prices the loan (interest rate)
4. Documentation: Loan agreement
5. Ongoing monitoring: Annual reviews
```

### Loan Pricing

```
LOAN PRICING (UK EXAMPLE)
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Base rate: SONIA (5.25% as of mid-2024)
+ Bank margin: 2.00%
= All-in rate: 7.25%

Margin depends on:
• Company credit quality (AAA vs B)
• Collateral (secured vs unsecured)
• Tenor (longer = higher rate)
• Relationship value
```

### Covenant Structures

```
LOAN COVENANTS
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Covenant = Rule the borrower must follow

Common covenants:
• Leverage: Net debt/EBITDA < 4.0x
• Interest cover: EBIT/interest > 3.0x
• Minimum liquidity: £X million
• Negative pledge: Can't encumber other assets
• Change of control: Cross-default

Breach = Technical default → Negotiation or call loan
```

## 💼 Syndicated Loans

### What Is a Syndicate?

When one borrower needs more than one bank can provide, multiple banks form a **syndicate**:

```
SYNDICATED LOAN STRUCTURE
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Borrower: £500M facility

Mandated Lead Arrangers:
• Barclays: £150M (agent bank)
• HSBC: £100M
• Lloyds: £100M
• NatWest: £100M
• Other participants: £50M

Roles:
• Arranger: Structures the loan
• Agent: Manages documentation, payments
• Participant: Lends their portion
```

### Syndications Process

```
LOAN SYNDICATION TIMELINE
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Week 1-2:  Mandate, term sheet, due diligence
Week 3-4:  Bank commit (MLAs sign up)
Week 5-6:  Documentation (legal)
Week 7:    Signing, funding
Week 8+:   Ongoing administration

The market is huge:
• Global syndicated loans: $10+ trillion/year
• UK: Major hub (London)
```

## 📊 Credit Analysis

### The 5 Cs of Credit

```
CREDIT ANALYSIS FRAMEWORK
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────
1. CHARACTER
   • Management quality and track record
   • Integrity, reputation
   • "Will they pay?"

2. CAPITAL
   • Equity cushion
   • Leverage levels
   • Capitalization

3. CAPACITY
   • Cash flow generation
   • Ability to service debt
   • Industry position

4. COLLATERAL
   • Assets securing the loan
   • Priority over other creditors
   • Quality of security

5. CONDITIONS
   • Economic environment
   • Industry outlook
   • Specific use of funds
```

### Key Credit Ratios

```
CREDIT METRICS
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Leverage:
• Debt/Equity = Total Debt / Shareholders' Equity
• Net Debt/EBITDA = (Debt - Cash) / Earnings
  → < 3x = healthy, > 5x = risky

Coverage:
• Interest Cover = EBIT / Interest Expense
  → > 3x = healthy, < 1.5x = risky

Liquidity:
• Current Ratio = Current Assets / Current Liabilities
  → > 1.5 = healthy

Profitability:
• EBITDA Margin = EBITDA / Revenue
  → Higher = better ability to service debt
```

### Credit Ratings

```
RATING AGENCIES
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Major agencies: Moody's, S&P, Fitch

Scale (Investment Grade):
• Aaa/AAA — Highest quality
• Aa/AA — Very high quality
• A/A — High quality
• Baa/BBB — Medium quality

Below Baa = "High Yield" / "Junk"
• Ba/BB — Speculative
• B/B — Highly speculative
• C/CCC — Default imminent
• D — In default
```

## ⚠️ Credit Risk

### Types of Credit Risk

```
CREDIT EVENT CATEGORIES
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────
1. DEFAULT
   • Borrower fails to pay
   • Technical or absolute

2. DOWNGRADE
   • Rating agency lowers rating
   • Triggers cross-default clauses

3. CREDIT SPREAD WIDENING
   • Market demands higher yield
   • Bond prices fall (even without default)

4. EARLY REPAYMENT
   • Borrower refinances
   • Lost income for lender
```

### Default Rates Through Time

```
HISTORICAL DEFAULT RATES
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• 2008 (Financial Crisis): HY bonds ~35% defaulted
• 2012 (Europe): ~5%
• 2020 (COVID): ~6% (then recovered fast)
• 2023: ~4% (elevated due to higher rates)

Recovery rates:
• Senior secured: 60-80%
• Senior unsecured: 40-60%
• Subordinated: 20-40%
```

### Credit Risk Management

```
MANAGING CREDIT EXPOSURE
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────
1. Diversification
   • Don't lend too much to one borrower
   • Industry/sector limits

2. Collateral
   • Security over assets
   • Guarantees/parent support

3. Monitoring
   • Annual credit reviews
   • Covenant testing
   • Market signals (ratings, spreads)

4. Hedging
   • Credit default swaps
   • Portfolio diversification
```

## 🏛️ Securitization

### What Is Securitization?

```
SECURITIZATION PROCESS
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────
1. Originator (bank) has many loans
   e.g., 10,000 mortgages

2. Pool loans into Special Purpose Vehicle (SPV)

3. SPV issues bonds (tranches)
   • Senior AAA tranche: First in line
   • Mezzanine: Middle risk/return
   • Equity tranche: Last in line, first loss

4. Investors buy tranches
   → Bank gets cash, removes loans from balance sheet
```

### Types of Securitization

```
SECURITIZATION EXAMPLES
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• RMBS (Residential Mortgage-Backed Securities)
  → UK: Very large market (NatWest, Barclays originate)
  → US: Enormous (Freddie Mac, Fannie Mae)

• CMBS (Commercial Mortgage-Backed Securities)
  → Office, retail, industrial properties

• Auto loan ABS
  → Car finance companies

• Credit card ABS
  → Consumer receivables

• CLOs (Collateralized Loan Obligations)
  → Corporate loans packaged together
```

### Securitization Controversies

```
2008 CRISIS LESSONS
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────
What went wrong:
• Subprime mortgages (bad loans) packaged
• Given high ratings (corruption + bad models)
• Sold to unsuspecting investors
• When housing crashed → massive defaults

Result:
• Hundreds of billions in losses
• Global financial crisis
• Reforms: Dodd-Frank, Basel III, rating agency reforms

→ Securitization isn't inherently evil, but needs scrutiny
```

## 💡 Key Takeaways

1. **Credit markets = Private debt** — Bank loans, syndicated financing, not traded bonds

2. **Largest debt market** — Much bigger than bond markets; most financing is bank-based

3. **Syndications** — Multiple banks share large loans; agent bank manages

4. **Credit analysis** — 5 Cs: Character, Capital, Capacity, Collateral, Conditions

5. **Key ratios** — Leverage (debt/EBITDA), Coverage (EBIT/interest), Liquidity

6. **Securitization** — Pooling loans into bonds; caused 2008 crisis when misused

7. **Credit risk is binary + spread risk** — Default or just higher yields hurt prices

   *(Answer: Debt/EBITDA — how many years of earnings to repay debt)*
