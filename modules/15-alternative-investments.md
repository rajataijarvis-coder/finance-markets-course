# Module 15: Alternative Investments

**Learning time:** 20-25 minutes  
**Prerequisites:** Modules 6-14 (Core markets understanding)

---

## 🤔 The Big Picture

**Alternative investments** are everything beyond traditional stocks and bonds. They include private equity, hedge funds, real estate, infrastructure, and collectibles.

Alternatives often promise higher returns but come with significant trade-offs: illiquidity, high fees, less transparency, and higher risk.

---

## 📖 What Makes Something "Alternative"?

### Key Characteristics

```
ALTERNATIVE VS TRADITIONAL
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────
┌───────────────────┬──────────────────┬──────────────────┐
│ Feature           │ Traditional     │ Alternative      │
├───────────────────┼──────────────────┼──────────────────┤
│ Liquidity        │ High (daily)     │ Low (years)      │
│ Transparency     │ High             │ Low              │
│ Regulation       │ Heavily          │ Less             │
│ Fees             │ Low (0.1-1%)     │ High (1-2%+      │
│                  │                  │  + performance)  │
│ Access           │ Easy             │ Often restricted │
│ Correlation      │ Known            │ Varies           │
└───────────────────┴──────────────────┴──────────────────┘
```

### Why Invest in Alternatives?

```
ALTERNATIVE DIVERSIFICATION
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Goal: Lower portfolio volatility through
assets that don't move with stocks/bonds

Potential benefits:
• Real assets (real estate, infra) → Inflation hedge
• Private equity → Alpha (excess returns)
• Hedge funds → Absolute returns
• Commodities → Inflation hedge

But: Higher fees, less liquidity, more risk
```

## 🏢 Private Equity (PE)

### What Is Private Equity?

```
PRIVATE EQUITY DEFINITION
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Private equity = Investing in private companies
(not listed on stock exchanges)

Types:
• Buyout: Buy entire companies, improve, sell
• Growth: Invest in scaling private companies
• Venture Capital: Early-stage startups
• Distressed: Buy troubled companies cheap

Examples of PE firms:
• UK/Europe: CVC, KKR Europe, Permira
• US: Blackstone, Carlyle, KKR, Silver Lake
```

### How PE Works

```
BUYOUT STRUCTURE
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────
1. PE firm raises fund from investors
   → "Limited Partners" (LPs) commit capital

2. PE buys company with:
   • Equity (from fund): ~30%
   • Debt (leveraged loan): ~70%
   → "Leveraged buyout" (LBO)

3. Improve company (3-7 years):
   • Operational improvements
   • Cost cutting
   • Acquisitions
   • Growth

4. Exit:
   • Sell to another PE firm (secondary)
   • IPO (rare)
   → Target: 2-3x returns
```

### Returns and Fees

```
PE FUND STRUCTURE
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Typical returns:
• Target IRR: 20%+ (ambitious)
• Realized returns: 15-20% historically

But fees are high:
• Management fee: 1.5-2% per year
• Performance fee: 20% of profits ("carried interest")
• First-dollar: Often 8% hurdle before carry

→ "2 and 20" is standard
→ Often costs 3-4% all-in per year
```

### Venture Capital (VC)

```
VC STAGES
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Seed: First money, ~£100K-1M
• Series A: Product-market fit, ~£1-5M
• Series B: Scaling, ~£5-20M
• Series C+: Growth, £20M+

Returns are extreme:
• Most startups fail (lose 100%)
• Some 10-100x returns
• Power law: Few deals drive all returns

UK VC examples:
• Seedcamp, Accel, Index Ventures
• Portfolio: Monzo, TransferWise, Farfetch
```

## 🦔 Hedge Funds

### What Is a Hedge Fund?

```
HEDGE FUND BASICS
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Hedge fund = Pooled investment vehicle that
can use many strategies (long, short, leverage, derivatives)

Goal: Absolute returns (positive returns in any market)
→ Not relative to an index

Key features:
• Long/short: Buy undervalued, short overvalued
• Leverage: Borrow to amplify returns
• Derivatives: Options, futures, swaps
• Performance fees: Often 20% of gains

Examples:
• UK: Winton, Man Group, Ochre Tree
• US: Bridgewater, Renaissance, Citadel
```

### Major Strategies

```
HEDGE FUND STRATEGIES
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────
1. LONG/SHORT EQUITY
   → Long best ideas, short worst
   → Net market exposure (often 50-80%)

2. MACRO
   → Bet on global macro themes
   → Currencies, rates, commodities
   → Examples: Ray Dalio (Bridgewater)

3. EVENT-DRIVEN
   → M&A arbitrage, distressed
   → Corporate actions profit

4. QUANTITATIVE
   → Computer-driven models
   → Statistical arbitrage, momentum

5. FIXED INCOME ARBITRAGE
   → Yield curve, spread trades
   → Lower risk, lower returns
```

### Performance

```
HEDGE FUND RETURNS
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Average returns (before fees): ~8-12% annually
After fees (2/20): ~5-8%

Issues:
• High fees eat returns
• Most underperform indices after fees
• Liquidity is limited (quarterly)
• Limited transparency

→ "Active management has a tough job"
  → Most can't beat passive after fees
```

## 🏠 Real Estate

### Direct vs Indirect Investment

```
REAL ESTATE INVESTING
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────
DIRECT:
• Buy property outright
• Full control, full risk
• Illiquid, management intensive

INDIRECT:
• REITs (Real Estate Investment Trusts)
• Property funds
• Listed real estate companies

UK REITs:
• British Land, Land Securities
• Unitt, Tritax Big Box
```

### Types of Real Estate

```
PROPERTY SECTORS
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Office: City centers, business parks
• Retail: Shopping centers, high street
• Industrial: Warehouses, logistics
• Residential: Housing, apartments
• Data centers: Cloud infrastructure
• Healthcare: Hospitals, care homes

Recent trends:
• Industrial ↑ (e-commerce)
• Office ↓ (hybrid work)
• Data centers ↑ (AI, cloud)
```

### Real Estate as an Investment

```
REAL ESTATE CHARACTERISTICS
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Potential benefits:
• Income: 4-8% dividend yield
• Appreciation: 3-5% long-term
• Inflation hedge: Rents rise with inflation
• Diversification: Low stock correlation

Risks:
• Illiquidity (years to sell)
• High transaction costs (5%+ fees)
• Management/maintenance
• Market cycles: Can drop 30%+

→ Best for long-term, patient capital
```

## 🚂 Infrastructure

### What Is Infrastructure?

```
INFRASTRUCTURE ASSETS
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Essential assets that society needs:
• Transport: Roads, airports, ports
• Utilities: Water, electricity, gas
• Social: Hospitals, schools, prisons
• Digital: Cell towers, fiber

UK examples:
• National Grid, Severn Trent
• Heathrow, Gatwick
• John Laing, International Public Partnerships
```

### Investment Characteristics

```
INFRASTRUCTURE AS AN ASSET CLASS
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Returns:
• Stable, predictable cash flows
• 5-10% annual returns (lower than PE)
• Often inflation-linked

Risks:
• Regulatory risk (government can change rules)
• Political risk (nationalization threats)
• Interest rate sensitivity

Why invest:
• Low correlation to stocks
• Inflation protection
• Stable income
```

## ⚠️ Key Risks in Alternatives

### 1. Illiquidity

```
LIQUIDITY RISK
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Private equity: Locked 7-10 years
Real estate: 3-5 years typical
Hedge funds: Quarterly at best

You might need money but can't access it!
→ Only invest what you can afford to lock up
```

### 2. High Fees

```
FEE IMPACT
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Traditional fund: 0.5% fee
Private equity: 2% management + 20% carry

Example:
• $100K invested, 10% return
• Traditional: $100K → $110K - $500 = $109.5K
• PE: $100K → $110K - $2K (mgmt) = $108K
  - $8K (carry) = $100K net

→ After fees, PE often underperforms
```

### 3. Fraud Risk

```
ALTERNATIVE SCAM RISK
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Less regulation = more fraud opportunity

Historical examples:
• Bernie Madoff: $65B fraud (hedge fund)
• Woodford: UK fund suspended (liquidity)
• Wirecard: Tech company fraud

→ Due diligence is critical
→ Only invest with trusted managers
```

## 💡 Key Takeaways

1. **Alternatives = Everything beyond stocks/bonds** — PE, hedge funds, real estate, infrastructure

2. **Illiquidity is the main trade-off** — Money locked up for years

3. **High fees are common** — 2+20 in PE, 2/20 in hedge funds

4. **Private equity** — Buy companies, improve, sell; target 20%+ returns

5. **Hedge funds** — Multiple strategies; aim for absolute returns

6. **Real estate** — Income + appreciation; inflation hedge

7. **Due diligence critical** — Less transparency, fraud risk higher
