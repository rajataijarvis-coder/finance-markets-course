## 🤔 Why Risk Management Matters

Here's an uncomfortable truth: **Most traders lose money.**

Not because they're stupid or lazy. Because they don't manage risk.

**The math:**

| Win Rate | Avg Win | Avg Loss | Result |
|----------|---------|----------|--------|
| 50% | $100 | $100 | Breakeven |
| 50% | $100 | $200 | Lose money |
| 40% | $200 | $100 | Breakeven |
| 60% | $100 | $150 | Lose money |

**The secret:** It's not about winning every trade. It's about:
- Winning more than you lose
- Making more on winners than you lose on losers

In this module, we'll cover:
- Position sizing
- Stop-loss placement
- Risk/reward ratios
- Managing multiple positions

## 📖 The Risk Management Framework

### The Golden Rules

1. **Never risk more than 1-2% per trade**
2. **Always use a stop-loss**
3. **Risk/reward must be positive**
4. **Size positions to your risk, not your conviction**

### Risk Per Trade

**Definition:** The amount you lose if the trade stops out.

**Example:**
```
Account: $10,000
Risk per trade: 2% = $200

If stop-loss = $50/share
Max shares = $200 / $50 = 4 shares

If stop-loss = 5% of price (e.g., $5 on $100 stock)
Max shares = $200 / $5 = 40 shares
```

> 💡 **Key insight:** The goal is not to avoid losses — it's to keep losses small so winners matter.

## 🛡️ Stop-Loss Strategies

### Types of Stops

**1. Fixed percentage stop:**
```
Always exit if position loses X%
Example: 5% stop on $100 stock = sell at $95
```

**2. Technical stop:**
```
Place stop at clear technical level
Example: Below support, below moving average
```

**3. Volatility stop:**
```
Place stop based on ATR (Average True Range)
Example: 2 × ATR below entry
```

**4. Time stop:**
```
Exit if trade doesn't work within X days
Example: Exit if no breakout in 5 days
```

### Where to Place Stops

**Ideal stop locations:**
- Just below support (for longs)
- Just above resistance (for shorts)
- Below significant wicks
- Outside obvious liquidity pools

**Bad stop locations:**
- Just inside support (obvious to everyone)
- Round numbers
- Where everyone puts their stops

### Mental vs. Fixed Stops

| Mental Stop | Fixed Stop |
|------------|------------|
| You remember the level | Platform automatically sells |
| Subject to delay | Instant execution |
| "I'll just wait" | No choice, exit is made |
| Dangerous | Safer |

**Recommendation:** Use fixed stops. Your brain will find excuses to not take the loss.

## 📊 Position Sizing

### The Formula

```
Position Size = (Account × Risk %) / (Entry - Stop Loss)
```

### Step-by-Step

**Step 1: Determine risk amount**
```
Account: $10,000
Risk: 2%
Risk amount = $10,000 × 0.02 = $200
```

**Step 2: Determine stop distance**
```
Entry: $100
Stop: $95
Stop distance = $5
```

**Step 3: Calculate position size**
```
Position = $200 / $5 = 40 shares
Total position = 40 × $100 = $4,000
```

### Position Sizing Examples

**Example 1: Tight stop**
```
Account: $10,000
Risk: 2% = $200
Entry: $100
Stop: $98 (2% away)
Position: $200 / $2 = 100 shares = $10,000 position
```

**Example 2: Wide stop**
```
Account: $10,000
Risk: 2% = $200
Entry: $100
Stop: $85 (15% away)
Position: $200 / $15 = 13 shares = $1,300 position
```

**Lesson:** Wider stops = smaller positions.

## ⚖️ Risk/Reward Ratio

### What is Risk/Reward?

```
Risk/Reward = (Entry - Stop) / (Target - Entry)
```

**Example:**
```
Entry: $100
Stop: $95 ($5 risk)
Target: $115 ($15 reward)
R/R = $5 / $15 = 1:3 (good!)
```

### Why It Matters

**With 1:1 risk/reward:**
- Need 50% win rate to break even
- 50% wins = lose money after costs

**With 1:2 risk/reward:**
- Need 33% win rate to break even
- 50% wins = solid profit

**The math:**
```
Trade 10 times, 5 wins, 5 losses, 1:2 R/R

Wins: 5 × $200 = $1,000
Losses: 5 × $100 = $500
Net: +$500
```

### Finding Proper Targets

**Method 1: Technical levels**
```
Target = Previous resistance / support
```

**Method 2: Measure move**
```
Swing size = $10 (from low to high)
Target = Entry + $10
```

**Method 3: Fixed multiple**
```
Target = Risk × 2 or 3
```

## 🎯 Risk Management in Practice

### The Trading Plan Template

**Before entering any trade, know:**

| Field | Example |
|-------|---------|
| Entry | $100 |
| Stop | $95 |
| Target | $115 |
| Risk | $5/share |
| Reward | $15/share |
| R/R | 1:3 |
| Position size | 40 shares ($4,000) |
| Max loss | $200 (2% of account) |

### Post-Trade Review

After each trade, ask:
1. Did I follow my rules?
2. Was the setup valid?
3. Did I manage risk correctly?
4. What can I learn?

### Track Your Statistics

**Keep a trading journal:**

| Date | Setup | Entry | Stop | Target | Result | R:R |
|------|-------|-------|------|--------|--------|-----|
| Jan 5 | Bounce | 100 | 95 | 115 | Win | 1:3 |
| Jan 8 | Breakout | 105 | 100 | 115 | Loss | 1:2 |
| Jan 12 | Pullback | 98 | 95 | 105 | Win | 1:2.3 |

**Metrics to track:**
- Win rate
- Average risk/reward
- Largest win / loss
- Profit factor (gross wins / gross losses)

## 🧠 The Psychology of Risk

### Why Losses Are Hard

- Loss aversion: Losses hurt more than wins feel good
- Ego: "I was wrong"
- FOMO: Fear of missing a recovery

### How to Accept Losses

1. **They're part of the game:** Even the best traders lose 40-50% of trades
2. **Small losses protect the account:** A 2% loss is recoverable; a 20% loss is devastating
3. **Wrong setup, right process:** You can lose on a good trade
4. **Stay in the game:** The next trade is what counts

### The Key Insight

> 💡 **The goal isn't to win every trade. It's to make more money than you lose.**

If you:
- Risk 2% per trade
- Have 1:2 risk/reward
- Win 40% of trades

Then over 10 trades:
- 4 wins × $200 = +$800
- 6 losses × $100 = -$600
- Net = +$200

**Even with more losses than wins, you're profitable.**

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes

### Mistake 1: No Stop-Loss
" It'll come back" → position doubles → account destroyed

### Mistake 2: Moving Stops
Moving stop to break even → stopped out → stock rallies

### Mistake 3: Oversizing
" I'm confident" → oversizing → blow up account

### Mistake 4: No Plan
"I' ll figure it out" → emotional decisions → losses

### Mistake 5: Revenge Trading
"I'm going to win back that loss" → doubling down → bigger loss

## 🔑 Key Takeaways

1. **The math:**
   - Risk management > winning rate
   - With 1:2 R/R, need only 33% win rate to profit

2. **Risk per trade:**
   - Never risk more than 1-2%
   - Small losses protect your account

3. **Stop-loss:**
   - Always use fixed stops
   - Place at technical levels
   - Below support for longs

4. **Position sizing:**
   - Calculate size based on risk amount
   - Wider stops = smaller positions

5. **Risk/reward:**
   - Always aim for 1:2 or better
   - Target should be at least 2× risk

6. **Trading plan:**
   - Know entry, stop, target before trading
   - Track statistics
