## 🤔 What is Sentiment Trading?

Markets aren't just driven by fundamentals. They're driven by **sentiment** — the collective mood of investors.

When sentiment is extreme (too bullish or too bearish), markets often reverse. This is trading against the crowd.

In this module, we'll cover:
- Understanding market sentiment
- Sentiment indicators
- Trading the news
- Using contrarian indicators

---

## 📖 Understanding Sentiment

### What is Market Sentiment?

**Definition:** The overall attitude of investors toward a particular market or asset.

```
Sentiment Spectrum:
─────────────────────────────
Very     Neutral    Very
Bearish              Bullish
```

### Why Sentiment Matters

**The principle:** Markets move from extreme to extreme.

```
Extreme bullish → Top (sell)
Extreme bearish → Bottom (buy)
```

**The pattern:**
1. Sentiment becomes extremely bullish
2. Everyone who wants to buy has bought
3. No more buyers = market tops
4. Sentiment becomes extremely bearish
5. Everyone who wants to sell has sold
6. No more sellers = market bottoms

> 💡 **Key insight:** The majority is usually wrong at extremes.

## 📊 Sentiment Indicators

### 1. VIX (Volatility Index)

**What it is:** The "fear index" — measures expected volatility of S&P 500

```
VIX Levels:
< 15:  Low fear (complacency)
15-20: Normal
20-30: Elevated fear
> 30:  High fear (panic)
```

**Trading with VIX:**
```
VIX < 15 + market at highs = warning (too complacent)
VIX > 30 + market at lows = opportunity (capitulation)
```

### 2. Put/Call Ratio

**What it is:** Ratio of put options to call options

```
P/C Ratio:
< 0.5: Too many calls (bullish, could be top)
> 1.5: Too many puts (bearish, could be bottom)
1.0:   Neutral
```

**Interpretation:**
- High puts = fear
- High calls = greed

### 3. AAII Sentiment Survey

**What it is:** Weekly survey of individual investor sentiment

```
Readings:
Bullish > 50%: Warning (crowd is bullish)
Bearish > 50%: Opportunity (crowd is bearish)
```

**The contrarian signal:**
- When bullish sentiment is extreme → sell
- When bearish sentiment is extreme → buy

### 4. Margin Debt

**What it is:** Amount of money borrowed to buy stocks

```
Rising margin debt = increasing leverage (risk)
Falling margin debt = deleveraging (safety)
```

**Trading signal:**
- Margin debt peaks near market tops
- Margin debt bottoms near market bottoms

### 5. Insider Buying/Selling

**What it is:** Executives buying or selling their own company's stock

```
Insider buying:  Bullish signal
Insider selling: Bearish signal
(when consistent over time)
```

## 📰 Trading the News

### Types of News

| Type | Example | Impact |
|------|---------|--------|
| Market-moving | Fed rate, earnings | Large |
| Sector | FDA approval, regulation | Sector-specific |
| Company | CEO change, product | Stock-specific |
| General | Economy, politics | Mixed |

### The News Trade

**Approach:**
1. News breaks
2. Assess impact
3. Identify direction
4. Enter with risk management

**Challenges:**
- News is already priced in
- Whipsaw volatility
- Hard to assess impact quickly

### Trading Strategies

**1. Fade the Initial Move**
```
News breaks → spike in one direction → fade back
Use when: Move seems overdone
Example: Earnings beat → stock rallies 10% → fade
```

**2. Ride the Move**
```
News breaks → confirm direction → ride
Use when: Real surprise, strong momentum
Example: Fed cuts rates unexpectedly → buy
```

**3. Wait for Consolidation**
```
News breaks → wait for price to settle → enter
Use when: Volatility too high
Example: Big news → wait 30 min, then enter
```

## 🎯 Contrarian Trading

### The Contrarian Principle

**Buy when others are fearful, sell when others are greedy.**

**Why it works:**
- Extreme sentiment = extreme positioning
- When everyone is long, no one is left to buy
- When everyone is short, no one is left to sell

### How to Be Contrarian

**Step 1: Measure sentiment**
- Use indicators above (VIX, P/C, surveys)
- Note extremes

**Step 2: Wait for confirmation**
- Sentiment extreme doesn't mean immediate reversal
- Wait for price to confirm

**Step 3: Position**
- Small position initially
- Add on pullbacks

**Step 4: Manage risk**
- Tight stops
- Expect volatility
- Give room

### Real Examples

**Example 1: March 2020 bottom**
```
VIX: 80 (extreme fear)
P/C ratio: 2.0+ (extreme puts)
Sentiment: Everyone saying "recession coming"
Result: Market bottomed and rallied 100%+
```

**Example 2: December 2021 top**
```
VIX: 19 (complacency)
P/C ratio: 0.4 (too many calls)
Sentiment: "Stocks only go up"
Result: 2022 bear market
```

## ⚠️ Sentiment Trading Risks

### 1. Sentiment Can Stay Extreme

Markets can stay irrational longer than you can stay solvent.

**Example:** "This time is different" can last years.

### 2. False Signals

Sentiment can be extreme without immediate reversal.

### 3. Confirmation Bias

You'll see what you want to see.

### 4. Timing is Everything

Being right but too early is the same as being wrong.

3. **News trading:**
   - Wait for impact assessment
   - Use appropriate strategy
   - Manage volatility

   - Buy fear, sell greed
   - Wait for confirmation
   - Use tight stops

5. **The principle:** Sentiment is a tool, not a guarantee
