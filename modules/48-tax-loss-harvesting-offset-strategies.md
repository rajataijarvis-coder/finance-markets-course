## 🤔 The Big Question

Your portfolio had a rough year. Some stocks dropped — maybe you held Tesla (TSLA) during a downtrend, or perhaps your European stocks got hit by economic uncertainty.

But here's the silver lining: **Those losses can reduce your tax bill.**

Welcome to **tax loss harvesting** — the art of turning losing investments into tax savings.

In this module, we'll cover:

## 📖 The Basics of Tax Loss Harvesting

### What It Is

**Tax loss harvesting** = selling investments at a loss to offset capital gains (or other income).

The goal: **Reduce your tax bill** while still maintaining market exposure.

```
Without harvesting:
- Gain on Apple (AAPL): £5,000
- Tax at 20%: £1,000

With harvesting:
- Gain on AAPL: £5,000
- Loss on Tesla (TSLA): £3,000
- Net gain: £2,000
- Tax at 20%: £400

You saved £600!
```

### Why It Works

Tax systems in most countries allow you to **net** gains against losses.

- **UK:** Losses can offset gains in the same year, or be carried forward
- **US:** Same — losses offset gains, excess can offset $3,000 of ordinary income
- **Europe:** Varies by country, but generally similar

## 🏴󠁧󠁢󠁥󠁮󠁧󠁿 UK: Tax Loss Harvesting

### UK Rules

**Key facts:**
- Losses can offset gains in the same tax year
- If losses exceed gains, carry forward indefinitely
- Losses must be **crystallised** (actually realized) by selling
- "Paper losses" don't count

### The UK Process

```
Step 1: Identify losing positions
        e.g., BP shares down 15%, Unilever down 8%

Step 2: Sell the losers
        e.g., Sell BP at £12.50 (was £18)

Step 3: Offset against gains
        e.g., Shell gain £5,000 - BP loss £2,750 = £2,250 taxable

Step 4: Reinvest (if desired)
        Buy similar but not "substantially identical" stock
```

### UK Example

```
Scenario:
- Sarah's portfolio in 2024/25
- Sold Shell (SHEL) profit: £8,000
- Sold BP (BP.) loss: £3,500
- Sold Unilever (ULVR) loss: £1,000
- Other gains: £2,000

Calculation:
- Total gains: £8,000 + £2,000 = £10,000
- Total losses: £3,500 + £1,000 = £4,500
- Net gain: £10,000 - £4,500 = £5,500

Tax (basic rate):
- Annual exemption: £3,000
- Taxable: £2,500
- Tax at 20%: £500

If she hadn't harvested:
- Tax on £10,000 = £2,000
- Savings: £1,500!
```

### What Counts as a Loss?

```
UK CGT LOSS RULES:
├── Shares that went down in value
├── Funds that underperformed
├── Business assets sold at loss
├── Cryptocurrency losses
└── Overseas losses (may be limited)
```

## 🇺🇸 US: Tax Loss Harvesting

### US Rules

**Key facts:**
- Losses offset gains dollar-for-dollar
- Excess losses offset up to $3,000 of ordinary income
- Remainder carries forward indefinitely
- **Wash sale rule** applies!

### The Wash Sale Rule (Critical!)

**The rule:** If you sell at a loss and buy the **same or substantially identical** security within **30 days before or after** the sale, you **cannot claim the loss**.

```
Example (Wash Sale):
- Jan 1: Buy 100 shares of NVDA at $500
- Feb 1: Sell 100 shares at $400 (realize $10,000 loss)
- Feb 15: Buy 100 shares back at $420 ← WASH SALE!

Result: You cannot claim the $10,000 loss.
It gets added to your new NVDA position's cost basis.
```

### How to Avoid Wash Sales

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│              AVOIDING WASH SALES                    │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│                                                     │
│  ✓ Buy a similar (but different) ETF:              │
│    • Sell SPY → buy VOO                            │
│    • Sell QQQ → buy VGT                            │
│                                                     │
│  ✓ Wait 31 days                                    │
│    • Sell on Jan 1 → buy back on Feb 2            │
│                                                     │
│  ✓ Buy in a different account                      │
│    • Sell in taxable → buy in IRA                 │
│    (IRA doesn't trigger wash sale)                │
│                                                     │
│  ✗ Don't buy the same ticker within 30 days       │
│                                                     │
│  ✗ Don't buy "substantially identical" securities │
│                                                     │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

### US Example

```
Scenario (2024):
- Mike's investments
- Sold Apple (AAPL) profit: $15,000
- Sold Tesla (TSLA) loss: $8,000
- Sold Netflix (NFLX) loss: $4,000

Calculation:
- Total gains: $15,000
- Total losses: $8,000 + $4,000 = $12,000
- Net: $15,000 - $12,000 = $3,000 gain

Tax (15% rate):
- $3,000 × 15% = $450

If he hadn't harvested:
- $15,000 × 15% = $2,250
- Savings: $1,800!
```

### Excess Loss Carryforward (US)

```
If losses > gains:
- Net loss can offset $3,000 of ordinary income
- Remainder carries forward forever

Example:
- Gains: $5,000
- Losses: $12,000
- Net loss: $7,000

Year 1:
- $3,000 offsets ordinary income
- $4,000 carries forward to next year
```

## 🇪🇺 Europe: Tax Loss Harvesting

### Germany

- Losses can offset gains in the same year
- Excess losses can carry forward **indefinitely**
- But: Losses from share sales can only offset **future gains from shares** (not other assets)
- Church tax may apply

### France

- Losses offset gains in the same year
- Carryforward: Up to **10 years** for shares
- PEA accounts: Special rules (losses within PEA can only offset gains within PEA)

### Netherlands

- Losses offset gains in the same year
- Carryforward: **indefinite**
- Box 3 system means this is less relevant (no actual CGT)

### Spain

- Losses offset gains in same year
- Carryforward: **4 years** only
- Must report losses within 4 years or they're lost

## 💡 Offset Strategies

### Strategy 1: Harvest Before Year-End

```
UK: Tax year ends 5 April
US: Tax year ends 31 December

Action: Review portfolio in March (UK) / December (US)
        Sell losers before year-end
        Offset gains
```

### Strategy 2: Offset Short-Term with Short-Term

```
US: Short-term losses first offset short-term gains
     (taxed at higher income rates)

UK: No distinction, but still matters for:
     • Annual exemption usage
     • Future carryforward
```

### Strategy 3: Use Losses Against Ordinary Income

```
US: After offsetting all gains,
    use up to $3,000 of loss against ordinary income
    
    This can save $600-$1,100+ depending on bracket
```

### Strategy 4: Plan for Future Gains

```
If you expect big gains soon:
• Hold losses in reserve
• Don't use them on small gains
• Wait for big gains to offset

Example:
• You have $10,000 loss banked
• You expect to sell appreciated stock next year
• Don't waste the loss on $2,000 of gains this year
```

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes

### ❌ Mistake 1: Harvesting Too Late

Review your portfolio **before** year-end, not in January.

### ❌ Mistake 2: Ignoring Transaction Costs

If the loss is smaller than trading fees, it's not worth it.

### ❌ Mistake 3: Violating Wash Sale Rule (US)

Don't rebuy the same stock within 30 days. Buy a similar ETF instead.

### ❌ Mistake 4: Forgetting Carryforward

Losses not used this year often carry forward. Track them!

### ❌ Mistake 5: Harvesting in Tax-Advantaged Accounts

**It doesn't work!** Losses in ISAs, 401ks, SIPPs, IRAs don't generate CGT deductions.

## 🔑 Key Takeaways

1. **Tax loss harvesting** = sell losing investments to offset gains
2. **UK:** Losses offset gains; excess carries forward indefinitely
3. **US:** Losses offset gains + up to $3,000 ordinary income; wash sale rule applies
4. **Europe:** Varies by country — check local rules
5. **Strategy:** Harvest before year-end; don't waste losses on small gains
6. **Critical (US):** Don't rebuy the same stock within 30 days
7. **Track carryforwards:** Losses not used now can reduce taxes later

## ✅ Quick Check

Test your understanding:

1. **UK Question:** Anna has £5,000 of losses and £2,000 of gains in the same tax year. What happens to the remaining £3,000 in losses?

2. **US Question:** You sell shares of Amazon at a $4,000 loss on January 15th. When can you buy Amazon shares again without triggering the wash sale rule?

3. **Strategy Question:** You have a choice: sell a losing position now to save $500 in taxes, or hold because you believe the stock will recover. What's the key consideration?

---

## Answers

1. **UK Answer:** The £3,000 loss **carries forward** indefinitely to offset future capital gains. Anna can use it in any future tax year.

2. **US Answer:** You must wait until **February 14th** (31 days after January 15th) to buy back Amazon shares. The wash sale rule covers the 30 days before AND after the sale.

3. **Strategy Answer:** Consider whether the **expected recovery** exceeds the **tax savings**. If you believe the stock will bounce back significantly, holding might be better. If it's a weak performer, harvesting makes sense. Also consider if you'd buy it back at current prices anyway.
